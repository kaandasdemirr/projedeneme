Projectile Motion Simulation ME 403 SEC4G4 2024 Fall

The purpose of this project is to evaluate and analyze the trajectory, velocity, and impact behavior of objects in projectile motion. By simulating various parameters—including launch angle, initial speed, and environmental factors—we aim to provide a comprehensive understanding of the dynamics involved in projectile motion. The data collected will be used to refine predictive models for projectile paths, enhancing applications in fields such as ballistics, sports science, and engineering.

This project serves as a practical tool for validating theoretical principles of motion, making it valuable for both educational and research settings. Users can reset the projectile to its initial position using the "Reset" button. The simulation can be started, stopped, and continued with a separate control button. For a more detailed observation, selecting the "Slow Motion" option allows the movement to proceed at one-tenth of the normal speed.

Users have the flexibility to vary several parameters, including initial height, initial speed, angle of inclination, mass, and gravitational acceleration, within certain limits. Radio buttons enable the selection of one of five physical dimensions for experimentation. Note that the effect of air resistance is neglected in this simulation to simplify the analysis.
